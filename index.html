How to Play Word Sudoku

To play the word sudoku games in this book, first select a game based on the vocabulary theme you want to practice. Then, identify the level and difficulty that is appropriate for your skills. Each word sudoku consists of a grid with spaces to fill in vocabulary words from the theme provided. Each row, column, and square in the grid must contain every word from the theme once, without repetition. Use the clues provided to figure out which word belongs in which space. The goal is to complete the entire grid with all the words from the theme without any repetition errors. It is possible your answers could be different from the sample answers given, and still correct. These games are a fun way to practice vocabulary and can be played individually or in groups. They are suitable for students, adults, and English language learners of all levels.

Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) Levels:

A1: A basic vocabulary of isolated words and phrases related to particular concrete situations.

A2: A basic vocabulary of isolated phrases and expressions concerning personal and concrete immediate needs. Can describe in simple terms aspects of their background, immediate environment and matters in areas of immediate need.

B1: An everyday vocabulary covering areas such as basic personal and family information, shopping, local geography, employment. Can use a range of simple and compound sentence structures, although errors and hesitation are often apparent.

B2: A wide range of vocabulary within the relevant field or discourse with an awareness of connotation. Can express themselves fluently and spontaneously, using discourse markers and connectors to link ideas together.

C1: A broad range of idiomatic expressions, colloquialisms and proverbial expressions. Can express themselves fluently and spontaneously, with a high degree of precision and naturalness.

C2: A very wide range of vocabulary, including idiomatic expressions and colloquialisms, showing complete awareness of register and style. Can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read, expressing themselves spontaneously, very fluently and precisely.
